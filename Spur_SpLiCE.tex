\begin{abstract}
Self-supervised visual encoders can exploit spurious correlations even when
class labels are absent from their standard pretraining objective. We study
whether sparse, language-aligned concepts extracted by a frozen
vision--language model can identify nuisance structure and guide the training
of a separate self-supervised encoder. Our pipeline decomposes frozen CLIP
embeddings with SpLiCE, automatically ranks concepts associated with a known
spurious attribute conditional on the target label, and injects the resulting
signal into SimCLR through either routed augmentation or class-conditional
correlation regularization. On Waterbirds, neither intervention yields a
reliable worst-group improvement over matched SimCLR. The strongest
broad-sweep configuration improves mean worst-group validation accuracy by
only $0.45$ points, with paired seed effects of $+2.38$, $+6.27$, and $-7.30$.
More decisively, Top-5 semantic routing underperforms a matched shuffled-routing
control on all three paired seeds by $1.64$, $0.32$, and $3.77$ points,
respectively. This result isolates a failure of the current scalar routing
interface, rather than establishing that concept representations are
intrinsically uninformative. Motivated by this distinction, we formalize
concept-conditioned contrastive positives as the next hypothesis: same-class
examples with dissimilar nuisance-concept profiles are treated as additional
positives, directly encoding the desired cross-background invariance. The
study provides a reproducible empirical diagnosis and a controlled transition
from concept-based sample routing to concept-conditioned representation
geometry.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Self-supervised learning (SSL) can learn transferable visual representations without target labels, but the resulting encoder may still exploit nuisance attributes that correlate with downstream labels. This creates a failure mode analogous to spurious correlation in supervised learning: average accuracy can remain high while performance on minority or conflict groups deteriorates.

We study whether an interpretable frozen vision--language model can expose such nuisance structure and convert it into a useful training signal for a separate SSL encoder. Our approach uses Sparse Linear Concept Embeddings (SpLiCE) \citep{bhalla2024splice} to approximate frozen CLIP image embeddings as sparse nonnegative combinations of text-concept embeddings. We automatically rank concepts according to their association with the known spurious attribute after conditioning on the target label. The resulting concept vectors are then used to (i) audit dataset bias, (ii) route selected images to a stronger SimCLR augmentation branch, or (iii) penalize class-conditional correlations between SSL features and selected concept coordinates.

The central empirical question is not whether SpLiCE produces plausible concept
names, but whether its per-image concept geometry induces useful invariances in
an independent encoder. We therefore compare semantic routing against matched
shuffled, random, and augment-all controls and evaluate worst-group (WG)
accuracy with paired seeds. The evidence is negative for the two implemented
interfaces: neither routed augmentation nor coordinate-wise correlation
regularization reliably improves WG accuracy, and semantic routing performs
worse than shuffled routing on each of three paired seeds. This controlled
failure localizes the current bottleneck. Reducing a concept vector to a scalar
augmentation decision discards the relational information needed to express
the desired invariance: examples with the same target semantics but different
nuisance content should be close in representation space.

This diagnosis motivates a distinct research hypothesis:
\emph{concept-conditioned contrastive positives}. Rather than using SpLiCE to
decide which individual images receive stronger augmentation, we use
dissimilarity between concept vectors to construct cross-image positive
relations. This formulation modifies the geometry optimized by contrastive
learning directly and admits matched shuffled-concept, random-positive, and
metadata-oracle controls.

Our current contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item an end-to-end pipeline that connects frozen SpLiCE concept discovery to the training of a separate SimCLR encoder;
    \item a target-conditioned concept-ranking procedure and a vector-valued correlation regularizer operating on per-image SpLiCE coordinates;
    \item a three-seed matched control showing that Top-5 semantic routing
    underperforms shuffled routing, isolating a failure of the current
    concept-to-augmentation interface;
    \item a reproducible protocol with RNG-isolated matched runs, cached decomposition scores, periodic probes, and explicit validation/test separation;
    \item an empirical diagnosis showing that plausible concept detection is
    insufficient for robust improvement under the two simple interventions
    studied here; and
    \item a falsifiable formulation that uses concept dissimilarity
    to mine same-class, cross-background positives, together with controls that
    distinguish semantic conditioning from the generic effect of adding
    positive pairs.
\end{itemize}

\section{Related Work}
\paragraph{Sparse concept decompositions.}
SpLiCE decomposes dense CLIP embeddings into sparse, nonnegative combinations of language-aligned concept embeddings and has been proposed for interpretation, concept bottlenecks, bias discovery, and representation editing \citep{bhalla2024splice}. In contrast, we freeze SpLiCE and use it as an external auditor and controller while training a separate ResNet encoder from scratch.

\paragraph{Robust self-supervised learning.}
Prior work has shown that SSL representations can encode and exploit spurious features, and has proposed feature-space augmentation or training-dynamics-based interventions \citep{hamidieh2024views,zhu2023lassl}. Our setting differs in the source of the intervention signal: rather than using the SSL encoder's internals or learning speed, we use human-readable nuisance coordinates extracted by a frozen vision--language model.

\paragraph{Concept discovery for spurious mitigation.}
Concept discovery has also been used to rebalance supervised classifier training. CoBalT, for example, learns dataset-specific object-centric latent concepts and uses them for concept-balanced sampling \citep{arefin2024cobalt}. Our objective is different: we investigate whether language-aligned concepts can directly shape the pretraining of a separate SSL representation rather than only the training distribution of a supervised classifier.

\section{Problem Setting}
Let each image $x_i$ have a target label $y_i$ and a spurious attribute $a_i$. The SSL encoder $f_\theta$ is trained with the SimCLR NT-Xent objective on two augmented views of each image \citep{chen2020simclr}. Target and spurious labels are not prediction targets of the SSL encoder, but the current concept-discovery and conditional-regularization variants use metadata during development. We therefore describe the method as \emph{metadata-assisted SSL}.

The main evaluation criterion is worst-group accuracy over groups defined by $(y,a)$. Hyperparameters are selected on validation data, and the test split is reserved for a final preselected configuration. A successful intervention should improve WG accuracy relative to a matched SimCLR baseline. A spurious-attribute probe is used as a diagnostic of linearly accessible nuisance information, but high probe accuracy alone is not treated as proof that the downstream classifier causally relies on the nuisance attribute.

\section{Method}
\subsection{Frozen SpLiCE decomposition}
A frozen CLIP encoder maps an image to a dense embedding. SpLiCE approximates this embedding using a sparse nonnegative vector over a vocabulary of text concepts. We use \texttt{open\_clip:ViT-B-32} with \texttt{laion2b\_s34b\_b79k} weights, the top 10,000 LAION vocabulary entries, and an $\ell_1$ penalty of $0.25$. The decomposition is computed once for the training split and cached.

For image $x_i$, let $c_i\in\mathbb{R}_{\geq0}^{K}$ denote the selected concept coordinates. The broad sweep uses ten concepts:
\begin{center}
\small\texttt{bamboo, forests, whale, rainforest, raven, forest, seascape, lake, snorkeling, pond}.
\end{center}
The routing-control series uses an automatically deduplicated and direction-consistent top five:
\begin{center}
\small\texttt{bamboo, forests, whale, rainforest, raven}.
\end{center}

\subsection{Automatic nuisance-concept ranking}
For concept $k$, target $y$, and spurious value $a$, let $\mu_{a,y,k}$ be the mean SpLiCE weight in that group. For binary attributes, we compute a target-conditioned spurious effect
\begin{equation}
S_k = \frac{1}{|\mathcal{Y}|}\sum_{y\in\mathcal{Y}}\left|\mu_{1,y,k}-\mu_{0,y,k}\right|,
\end{equation}
a spurious-conditioned target effect
\begin{equation}
T_k = \frac{1}{|\mathcal{A}|}\sum_{a\in\mathcal{A}}\left|\mu_{a,1,k}-\mu_{a,0,k}\right|,
\end{equation}
and an instability term that penalizes disagreement in the magnitude of the spurious effect across target classes. With unit coefficients, the ranking score is
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Score}_k = S_k-T_k-I_k.
\end{equation}
The revised control series additionally requires the signed effect $d_{k,y}=\mu_{1,y,k}-\mu_{0,y,k}$ to have the same direction for every target class and removes simple lexical duplicates before selecting the top $K$ concepts.

\subsection{Concept-routed augmentation}
For routing, the selected vector is reduced to
\begin{equation}
r(x_i)=\max_k c_{ik}.
\end{equation}
A threshold is set by a training-set quantile $q$. Images above the threshold produce one standard and one strong view,
\begin{equation}
\left[T_{\mathrm{std}}(x),T_{\mathrm{strong}}(x)\right],
\end{equation}
whereas images below the threshold produce two standard views. At $q=0.75$, semantic, shuffled, and matched-random policies each route exactly 1,199 of 4,795 training images (25.01\%). The augment-all control routes every image.

\subsection{Conditional correlation regularization}
The SSL encoder produces $h_i=f_\theta(x_i)\in\mathbb{R}^{d}$. Because target and spurious attributes are correlated in the biased training set, embeddings and concept vectors are centered separately within each target class:
\begin{equation}
\widetilde h_i=h_i-\frac{1}{|B_{y_i}|}\sum_{j\in B_{y_i}}h_j,
\qquad
\widetilde c_i=c_i-\frac{1}{|B_{y_i}|}\sum_{j\in B_{y_i}}c_j.
\end{equation}
The feature--concept correlation matrix is
\begin{equation}
R_{jk}=\frac{\sum_i\widetilde h_{ij}\widetilde c_{ik}}
{\sqrt{\sum_i\widetilde h_{ij}^{2}}\sqrt{\sum_i\widetilde c_{ik}^{2}}+\epsilon},
\end{equation}
and the regularized objective is
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}=\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{SimCLR}}+
\lambda\frac{1}{dK}\sum_{j=1}^{d}\sum_{k=1}^{K}R_{jk}^{2}.
\end{equation}
This penalty discourages linear accessibility of each selected concept coordinate within a fixed target class, but does not guarantee removal of nonlinear or distributed nuisance information.

\subsection{Concept-conditioned contrastive positives}
\label{sec:concept_positives}
The routing intervention above compresses $c_i$ to a single score and uses that
score only to select an augmentation policy. Our control results motivate a
different interface between SpLiCE and the SSL objective: preserve the
vector-valued relation between examples and use it to select additional
positives. We present this objective as a falsifiable hypothesis; it is not
included among the methods evaluated in the current results.

Let
$\bar c_i=c_i/\max(\lVert c_i\rVert_2,\epsilon_c)$ be the normalized vector of
selected nuisance-concept coordinates, and define concept dissimilarity
\begin{equation}
d_c(i,j)=1-\bar c_i^\top\bar c_j.
\end{equation}
For anchor $i$, we restrict candidates to examples with the same target label
and select the $m$ most concept-dissimilar examples,
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{P}^{\mathrm{cc}}_i
=
\operatorname{TopM}_{j\neq i:\,y_j=y_i}
d_c(i,j).
\label{eq:cc_positive_set}
\end{equation}
This rule operationalizes the intended invariance: preserve target semantics
while varying the concept profile associated with the background. In the
minimal implementation, candidates are drawn from the current minibatch and
the cached SpLiCE vectors are detached from gradient computation. Anchors
without a valid same-class candidate are omitted from
$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{cc}}$.

Let $z_i$ denote the normalized SimCLR projection of an augmented view of
$x_i$. We add a multi-positive contrastive term over the mined positives,
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{cc}}
=
-\frac{1}{|B|}
\sum_{i\in B}
\frac{1}{|\mathcal{P}^{\mathrm{cc}}_i|}
\sum_{p\in\mathcal{P}^{\mathrm{cc}}_i}
\log
\frac{\exp(z_i^\top z_p/\tau_{\mathrm{cc}})}
{\sum_{a\in B\setminus\{i\}}\exp(z_i^\top z_a/\tau_{\mathrm{cc}})},
\end{equation}
and optimize
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}
=
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{SimCLR}}+\alpha\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{cc}}.
\label{eq:cc_objective}
\end{equation}
Because candidate positives are restricted using $y$, this formulation is
metadata-assisted rather than fully self-supervised.

Three controls are necessary for attribution. A \emph{within-class shuffled}
control permutes concept vectors inside each target class before mining,
preserving the positive count and label structure while destroying the
semantic assignment. A \emph{random same-class} control measures the benefit
of adding cross-image positives without concept conditioning. Finally, an
\emph{oracle cross-background} control uses the known spurious attribute only
for diagnosis and estimates whether the objective can help when positive
selection is correct. Together, these controls distinguish failures of concept
discovery from failures of the contrastive intervention.

\section{Experimental Setup}
\subsection{Dataset and evaluation}
We evaluate on Waterbirds. A ResNet18-large encoder is trained with SimCLR for 1,000 epochs. \subsection{Reproducibility controls}
Matched baseline and SpLiCE runs start from the same SSL model initialization. Frozen CLIP/SpLiCE loading, W\&B initialization, and periodic probes are RNG-isolated. Persistent checkpoints store Python, NumPy, CPU Torch, CUDA, DataLoader-generator, and AMP-scaler states. Validation and final-test entry points are separated so that development runs cannot accidentally select on the test set.

\subsection{Semantic routing controls}
The routing-control experiment tests whether the per-image semantic assignment
produced by SpLiCE matters, rather than merely applying an extra augmentation
to one quarter of the dataset. The semantic--shuffled comparison is now
complete for seeds 1, 3, and 4. Semantic routing obtains
$44.49\pm4.92\%$ WG accuracy, compared with $46.40\pm3.24\%$ for shuffled
routing. More importantly, the paired semantic-minus-shuffled differences are
$-1.64$, $-0.32$, and $-3.77$ points, yielding a mean paired difference of
$-1.91$ points with the same sign on every observed seed. On seed 4, semantic
routing also reduces average accuracy from $50.04\%$ to $47.81\%$.

The seed-4 pair was trained on the same home RTX 5080 under identical software
and routed exactly 1,199 images in both conditions. Its within-pair comparison
is therefore insulated from the hardware change relative to the existing
seed-4 baseline. The three-seed sample remains too small for a strong
inferential claim, but the current Top-5 scalar semantic router fails its
matched shuffled control on every observed seed. This conclusion is
deliberately narrower than claiming that the selected concept vectors contain
no useful nuisance information.

\subsection{Strong-augmentation components}
Only the all-components configuration has three comparable post-repair seeds, and it does not outperform baseline. Single-component crop, color-jitter, grayscale, and Gaussian-blur results currently have only one comparable seed each and therefore cannot support a stability claim. Spurious-attribute probe accuracy remains high across these runs (approximately 90.40--92.49\% average validation accuracy for seed 1), so the current augmentation policy does not provide evidence that background information has ceased to be linearly accessible.

\section{Discussion}
\paragraph{What the negative control establishes.}
The experiments expose a distinction between \emph{detecting} nuisance
concepts and \emph{using} them to induce invariance. SpLiCE produces
human-readable coordinates associated with the Waterbirds background, but a
large scalar concept score is not a prescription for how an individual image
should be transformed. Strong generic transforms may preserve the nuisance,
damage the foreground, or make instance discrimination unnecessarily
difficult. The all-negative semantic-minus-shuffled paired differences provide
direct evidence against the specific routing interface: for a fixed
augmentation budget, the semantic assignment is not better than destroying
that assignment. The experiment does not, however, test whether pairwise
relations between the concept vectors are useful.

\paragraph{Why the next hypothesis is relational.}
Spurious robustness is fundamentally a statement about representation
geometry. Examples that share target semantics should remain close even when
their nuisance attributes differ. Neither implemented intervention expresses
this relation directly. Routing acts independently on each example after
reducing $c_i$ to $\max_k c_{ik}$, while correlation regularization suppresses
marginal linear dependence without specifying which examples should align.
Concept-conditioned positives in Section~\ref{sec:concept_positives} retain the
vector-valued concept relation and place it inside the contrastive objective.
This is a substantive change in mechanism, not another hyperparameterization
of routed augmentation.

\paragraph{Evaluation protocol for the proposed objective.}
The proposed objective should not be credited to concept conditioning merely
because it outperforms SimCLR. It must outperform a random same-class positive
baseline and a within-class shuffled-concept control under matched positive
counts and initializations. The oracle cross-background condition provides a
separate diagnostic: if the oracle fails, the contrastive intervention is
mis-specified; if the oracle succeeds but semantic mining fails, concept
discovery or its distance metric is the bottleneck. Only a semantic advantage
over both non-semantic controls, accompanied by improved WG accuracy and
reduced spurious-probe accuracy across paired seeds, would support the stronger
claim that SpLiCE contributes useful training geometry.

\section{Limitations}
The present study is restricted to Waterbirds, and three post-repair seeds are
insufficient for strong statistical claims. Although the semantic--shuffled
comparison is complete, the random and augment-all routing controls remain
partial. The seed-4 semantic--shuffled pair shares hardware and software, but
the existing seed-4 SimCLR baseline was trained on the cluster; causal
interpretation should therefore prioritize the within-pair routing comparison.
Concept discovery uses target and spurious metadata, correlation
regularization uses target labels for conditional centering, and the proposed
positive-mining rule restricts candidates by target label. These variants are
metadata-assisted rather than group-agnostic SSL. Selected concepts such as
\texttt{whale} and \texttt{raven} may be imperfect proxies for background
structure, and cosine distance over a small selected vocabulary may not capture
all nuisance variation. Finally, linear leakage probes measure accessibility
rather than causal use, and no revised method has yet been evaluated on the
held-out test set.

\section{Conclusion}
We presented a reproducible framework for connecting frozen SpLiCE concepts to
the training and evaluation of a separate self-supervised encoder. On
Waterbirds, neither concept-routed augmentation nor target-conditioned
correlation regularization yields a reliable WG improvement over matched
SimCLR. The completed three-seed control is more diagnostic: semantic Top-5
routing underperforms shuffled routing on every paired seed. This negative
result rules out the current scalar routing mechanism, but not the broader
hypothesis that concept geometry can guide robust representation learning. It
therefore redirects the investigation from concept-conditioned augmentation to
concept-conditioned contrastive relations. A decisive evaluation must mine
same-class, cross-background positives from vector-valued SpLiCE profiles and
compare them against shuffled-concept, random-positive, and oracle controls.

\section{Reproduction Notes}
The main entry point is \texttt{spur\_splice.py}. Supported SpLiCE modes are \texttt{none}, \texttt{augment}, \texttt{corr\_reg}, and \texttt{augment\_corr\_reg}. Utility scripts include \texttt{tools/discover\_splice\_spurious\_concepts.py} for group-conditioned concept ranking and \texttt{tools/summarize\_splice\_scores.py} for score-distribution summaries. The same experiment arrays accept \texttt{DATASET=spur\_cifar10} and automatically select a standard ResNet18 for $32\times32$ inputs. Final test evaluation requires the explicit \texttt{--final\_test} flag.
